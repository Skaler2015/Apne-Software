ℹ️ About This Tool

Pages don't always end up in the right order, especially in documents built from scanned paper, combined from multiple sources, or assembled by different people working on different sections at different times. Maybe the second half of a contract got scanned before the first half, a chapter ended up after its appendix, or a set of forms needs to follow a specific sequence required by whoever is reviewing them. Rather than starting over with a fresh scan or rebuilding the document from scratch, this tool lets you simply drag pages into whatever order you need, directly inside your browser.

After you upload a PDF, every page appears as a thumbnail you can see and recognize at a glance, arranged in a grid that reflects the current order. To fix the sequence, you just click and drag any thumbnail to its new position — the rest of the pages shift around it automatically, and you can keep adjusting until the order looks exactly right. This visual, drag-and-drop approach is far more reliable than trying to describe a new page order in numbers, since you're looking at the actual content of each page rather than guessing from a page count, which removes the kind of off-by-one mistake that's easy to make when juggling page numbers in your head.

This comes up constantly with real documents: legal contracts where exhibits need to follow specific clauses in a particular sequence, academic papers where figures and appendices have a required placement, scanned multi-page forms where sheets were fed into a scanner out of order, or combined reports where sections from different contributors need to be interleaved correctly before final submission. Getting the order right matters for documents that will be reviewed formally, since pages out of sequence can make even well-written content look careless or incomplete.

As with the rest of this site's PDF tools, reordering happens entirely within your browser using client-side processing — your document is never uploaded to a server just to rearrange its pages, which matters if you're working with anything confidential like legal filings or financial records. Once you're satisfied with the new sequence, a single click saves the reordered PDF, ready to download immediately, with every page's original content, formatting, and quality completely untouched — only the order has changed. There's no page limit, no watermark, and no account needed, so it works equally well whether you're swapping two pages in a short document or rearranging a hundred-page file section by section.

How to use:

  1. Upload your PDF.
  2. Use the arrow buttons to move pages into the order you want.
  3. Download the PDF in the new order.
