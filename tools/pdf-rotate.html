🔃 PDF Rotate

Rotate the pages of a PDF — all pages at once, or one at a time.

📤 Drag & drop your PDF file here or click to choose

ℹ️ About This Tool

A sideways or upside-down page is one of the most common small annoyances with scanned PDFs — it happens when a document is fed into a scanner the wrong way, when a phone is held in the wrong orientation while photographing pages, or when pages from different sources with different orientations get combined into one file. Rather than re-scanning the document or living with a page you have to tilt your head to read, this tool lets you rotate any page, or every page, of a PDF by 90, 180, or 270 degrees in just a couple of clicks.

You can apply a single rotation to the entire document at once if every page has the same orientation problem, which is the most common case with a document that was simply scanned sideways from start to finish. Alternatively, you can go page by page and rotate only the specific pages that need it, leaving correctly oriented pages untouched — useful for documents assembled from multiple sources where only a few pages came in rotated while the rest were fine. A live preview shows you exactly how each page will look after rotation before you finalize anything, so there's no guesswork and no need to download a test version just to check whether you got the right angle.

This comes up constantly with documents scanned on shared office scanners or multifunction printers, where it's easy for a page to get fed in slightly wrong, especially with double-sided scanning or mixed-orientation source documents like a mix of portrait letters and landscape spreadsheets. Fixing this after the fact with this tool is far faster than walking back to the scanner, finding the original paper, and redoing the entire scan just because of one or two misaligned pages.

As with the other PDF tools here, rotation happens entirely within your browser; the file is processed locally and never uploaded to a remote server, so there's no waiting on an upload or download round-trip and no concern about where a copy of your document might end up. Once you're satisfied with the orientation of every page, a single click saves the corrected PDF, ready to download, print, or share looking exactly the way it should. There's no limit on how many pages you can fix in one pass, no watermark added to the output, and no account or sign-up required, so it works just as well for a one-page certificate as it does for a hundred-page scanned report with orientation problems scattered throughout.

How to use:

  1. Upload your PDF.
  2. Rotate individual pages or all pages using the rotate buttons.
  3. Save and download the corrected PDF.
